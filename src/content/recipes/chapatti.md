The simplest flatbread: flour, water, salt. No yeast, no oil in the dough, no drama.

## Ingredients

- 150g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
- A pinch of salt
- About 90ml water

## Method

### 1. Make the dough

Put the flour and salt in a bowl. Add the water gradually, mixing as you go, until it comes together into a soft dough.

If it is sticky, add a little more flour. If it feels tight or crumbly, add a splash more water.

### 2. Knead and rest

Knead for a few minutes until smooth.

Cover and rest for 20 to 30 minutes.

### 3. Roll

Divide the dough into small balls.

Roll each one out on a lightly floured surface, turning as you go, until thin and roughly round. Roughly round is fine. Dinner will survive.

### 4. Cook

Heat a dry frying pan or tawa until hot.

Cook each chapatti for a minute or so, until bubbles appear and the underside has brown spots. Flip and cook the other side.

Press gently with a clean cloth or spatula to help it puff in places.

## No-faff notes

- Plain flour works. Chapatti flour or atta is better if you have it.
- Keep cooked chapattis wrapped in a clean tea towel so they stay soft.
- Brush with a little butter or ghee if you want them richer.
