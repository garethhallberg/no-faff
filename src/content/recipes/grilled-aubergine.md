Aubergine done properly. Smoky from the pan, soft in the middle, sharp from the dressing.

## Ingredients

- 1 large aubergine
- 2 cloves garlic, finely sliced
- Olive oil
- Salt

For the dressing:

- Juice of 1 lemon
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- A small handful of dill, chopped

## Method

### 1. Slice and oil

Put the olive oil and finely sliced garlic in a small bowl. Let it sit for 10 minutes while you slice the aubergine into 6 thick rounds.

Brush or rub the aubergine with the garlic-infused oil, but don’t transfer the garlic slices onto the pan as they'll burn and become bitter.

A pinch of salt.

### 2. Get the pan hot

Grill pan over a moderate heat. Not blazing — moderate. You want time for the inside to cook before the outside burns.

### 3. Grill in batches

Add the aubergine slices a few at a time. Don't crowd the pan.

A few minutes each side, until you've got proper dark grill marks and the aubergine is soft when you press it.

### 4. Dress

Whisk the lemon juice, olive oil, the garlic from earlier and dill together with a pinch of salt.

Pile the warm aubergine onto a plate. Spoon the dressing over.

## Why this works

Aubergine without enough oil is squeaky and disappointing. Aubergine with garlic oil, then grilled, then dressed with lemon, is one of the great everyday dinners.

The lemon cuts through the richness. The dill keeps it fresh.

## Make it immense

- Crumbled feta on top.
- A spoonful of yoghurt underneath.
- Pomegranate seeds for crunch and pop.
- Toasted pine nuts.

## No-faff swaps

- No grill pan? A heavy frying pan works. You won't get the bar marks but you'll get the colour.
- No dill? Mint. Parsley. Both at once.
- No fresh lemon? Don't bother. Use vinegar and a different recipe.
