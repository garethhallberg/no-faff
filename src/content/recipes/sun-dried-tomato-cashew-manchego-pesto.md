Somewhere near pesto alla Trapanese, but heading off in its own direction. I first made this when I wanted pesto but had no parmesan or pine nuts or even basil so I did some swaps. It worked a treat.

Sun-dried tomato, cashew, Manchego, garlic, lemon, and plenty of extra virgin olive oil. Rough, salty, sweet, chewy, crunchy. Very good on properly toasted bread.

## Ingredients

- 1 small garlic clove
- A pinch of salt
- 1 small handful cashews
- 5 to 6 sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
- A little Manchego, grated
- Black pepper
- A tiny squeeze of lemon
- Plenty of extra virgin olive oil
- 1 to 2 teaspoons oil from the sun-dried tomato jar, optional
- Good bread, for toast

## Method

### 1. Garlic

Put the garlic clove and a pinch of salt into a pestle and mortar.

Smash it into a paste.

### 2. Cashews

Add the cashews and bash them into the garlic.

Keep some texture. You want crunchy cashew bits, not nut butter.

### 3. Tomatoes

Add the chopped sun-dried tomatoes and work them in.

They should break down a bit, but still stay chewy.

### 4. Cheese

Stir in a little grated Manchego, black pepper, a tiny squeeze of lemon, and a good glug of extra virgin olive oil.

Add 1 to 2 teaspoons of the tomato jar oil too if you want more of that deep tomato flavour.

Keep adding extra virgin olive oil until it looks glossy and spoonable.

### 5. Toast

Toast the bread properly.

If you want more garlic, rub the hot toast lightly with raw garlic before spreading. If the mix is already garlicky enough, skip it.

### 6. Finish

Spread the pesto onto the toast.

Finish with a bit more grated Manchego and a few drops of lemon or tomato oil.

## Sharp side salad

This wants something fresh and sharp next to it because the toast is rich.

Toss rocket, thinly sliced fennel or cucumber, a few parsley leaves, lemon juice, extra virgin olive oil, salt and black pepper. Keep it bright. No sweetness needed.

## Why this works

Sun-dried tomatoes bring deep sweetness and chew. Cashews bring crunch and body. Manchego brings salty, nutty richness. Lemon stops it getting heavy, and extra virgin olive oil makes it glossy, loose and properly pesto-like.

The tomato jar oil is good, but it is a seasoning here. The pesto still wants proper olive oil.

The whole point is texture. Stop before it becomes baby food.

## Make it immense

- Toast the cashews first.
- Add a tiny pinch of chilli flakes.
- Use a little lemon zest as well as juice.
- Finish with chopped parsley or basil.

## No-faff swaps

- No Manchego? Parmesan, pecorino or mature cheddar will still be good.
- No cashews? Almonds are closer to Trapanese territory. Walnuts work too.
- No pestle and mortar? Chop everything finely by hand and mash it together with the side of a knife.
- Too salty? More lemon and a few more cashews.

## Eat with

- Toast
- Crackers
- Roast peppers
- Grilled aubergine
- A sharp green salad
