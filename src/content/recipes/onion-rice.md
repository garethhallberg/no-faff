This is the onion version of the basic basmati method: **80g rice per person, then double that in cold water**.

The trick is giving the onions enough time to soften and sweeten. Pale, barely cooked onion is not the point here.

## Ingredients

- 160g basmati rice
- 320ml cold water
- 2 onions, finely sliced
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 tablespoon butter
- Salt
- Black pepper
- A small handful of parsley or chives, chopped (optional)

## Method

### 1. Measure the rice

Use 80g basmati rice per serving.

For two servings, put 160g rice in a saucepan with 320ml cold water and a pinch of salt.

### 2. Cook it

Bring the pan to the boil.

As soon as it boils, put the lid on, turn the heat right down, and cook for 10 minutes.

Leave it alone while it cooks.

### 3. Cook the onions

While the rice cooks, warm the olive oil and butter in a frying pan over a medium heat.

Add the onions with a pinch of salt and cook for 12 to 15 minutes, stirring now and then, until soft, golden and sweet.

If they start catching before they soften, turn the heat down.

### 4. Rest the rice

Turn off the heat under the rice and leave it, lid still on, for a couple of minutes.

Fluff it with a fork.

### 5. Finish

Fold the onions through the rice.

Add plenty of black pepper, herbs if you have them, and taste for salt.

## Why this works

The rice cooks cleanly by absorption, while the onions get their own time in the pan. Salt helps them soften, butter makes them taste rounded, and black pepper stops the whole thing becoming too sweet.

## Make it immense

- Add a bay leaf to the rice while it cooks.
- Cook the onions with a pinch of cumin seeds.
- Finish with crispy onions on top.
- Add a squeeze of lemon if it needs lifting.

## No-faff swaps

- Brown, white or red onions all work.
- No butter? Use more olive oil.
- Want it richer? Cook the onions slower and darker.
- Want it fresher? Add chopped herbs at the end.
