Roast the cauliflower first. It makes the whole thing taste deeper, and the fenugreek in the oil gives it a quiet savoury warmth before the cheese sauce even gets involved.

## Ingredients

- 1 large cauliflower, broken into florets
- 2 tablespoons neutral oil or olive oil
- 1 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
- 40g butter
- 40g plain flour
- 500ml milk
- 100g mature cheddar, grated
- 50g Red Leicester, grated
- 25g Parmesan, finely grated
- Salt and pepper

## Method

### 1. Make the fenugreek oil

Warm the oil gently with the fenugreek seeds for a few minutes, just until fragrant.

Take it off the heat and let it sit while you prepare the cauliflower.

### 2. Roast the cauliflower

Heat the oven to 200C.

Brush the cauliflower florets with the fenugreek oil, then spread them out on a baking tray. Season with salt and pepper.

Roast for 20 to 25 minutes, until the edges are browned and the florets are just tender.

### 3. Make the cheese sauce

Melt the butter in a saucepan over a medium heat.

Stir in the flour and cook for a minute or two, keeping it moving.

Add the milk gradually, a little at a time, stirring well after each addition so the sauce stays smooth.

Once all the milk is in, simmer until thickened.

Stir in the cheddar, Red Leicester and Parmesan until melted. Taste and season with salt and pepper.

### 4. Bake

Turn the oven down to 180C.

Mix the roasted cauliflower and cheese sauce together in an ovenproof dish.

Bake for 20 minutes, until bubbling and golden on top.

## No-faff notes

- Mustard in the sauce is welcome, but not compulsory.
- A bit more Red Leicester on top before baking gives it proper colour.
- If the sauce gets too thick, loosen it with a splash more milk.
