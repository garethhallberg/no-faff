Fennel that has been roasted properly is one of the best things you can put in your mouth. Sweet, slightly burnt at the edges, perfumed with lemon.

## Ingredients

- 2 fennel bulbs, cut into wedges (keep the fronds)
- 6 shallots, peeled and halved
- A handful of black olives
- 2 lemons (yes, two — one sliced, one for juice)
- Olive oil
- Salt and pepper

## Method

### 1. Heat the oven

190°C.

### 2. Par-boil the fennel

Boil the fennel wedges for 5 minutes in well-salted water. Drain.

This gives them a head start so they don't dry out before they caramelise.

### 3. Build the tin

Transfer the fennel to a roasting tin. Add the shallots, the olives, and one of the lemons sliced into thin rounds.

Toss in just enough olive oil to coat. Salt and pepper. Squeeze over the juice of the second lemon.

Yeah. We're using two lemons. Don't argue.

### 4. Roast

45 minutes. We want serious caramelisation — the edges should be properly dark, the shallots collapsing, the lemon slices going chewy and slightly burnt.

### 5. Finish

Sprinkle the reserved fennel fronds over the top to serve.

For the win.

## Why this works

The boil softens the fennel so it has time in the oven to colour without going dry. The shallots melt down into something close to jam. The roasted lemon is the magic — bittersweet, almost like a preserved lemon, and worth the second one.

## Make it immense

- A drizzle of tahini after roasting.
- White beans tossed in for the last 10 minutes — turns it into a dinner.
- Crumbled goat's cheese.

## No-faff swaps

- No shallots? Quartered red onion.
- No black olives? Green olives. Capers. Anything salty.
- One lemon if you really must. But two is better. We covered this.
