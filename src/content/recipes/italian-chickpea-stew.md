This is lunch made from almost nothing.

Onions, garlic, chickpeas, water, patience. Then bread, potatoes or greens alongside.

## Ingredients

- 2 onions, chopped
- A few cloves of garlic, chopped
- 1 large jar of chickpeas, around 800g
- Half a jar of water
- Extra virgin olive oil
- Salt and black pepper

## Method

### 1. Onions

Warm a good glug of extra virgin olive oil in a large pan.

Add the chopped onions and cook gently for about 8 minutes, until translucent.

You want soft and sweet, not brown and shouty.

### 2. Garlic

Add the chopped garlic and cook for another minute or two.

Do not let it burn.

### 3. Chickpeas

Add the whole jar of chickpeas to the pan.

Fill the jar halfway with water, swill it around, and add that too.

Season with salt and black pepper.

### 4. Simmer

Simmer gently for 45 minutes.

Stir now and then. If it gets too thick, add a splash more water.

### 5. Serve

Serve with potatoes, bread or some greens.

That is lunch sorted.

## Why this works

The onions give sweetness, the garlic gives depth, and the chickpeas turn soft and creamy as they simmer.

There is no cleverness here. Just enough time for simple ingredients to become something better.

## Make it immense

- Finish with a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil.
- Add rosemary or sage with the garlic.
- Stir through a handful of chopped parsley at the end.
- Grated parmesan or pecorino on top.

## No-faff swaps

- No onions? Use a leek.
- No jarred chickpeas? Use two cans.
- Want it soupier? Add more water.
- Want it thicker? Crush some of the chickpeas with the back of a spoon.
- No bread or potatoes? Serve it with greens, or eat it as it is.
