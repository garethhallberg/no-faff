Flour, water, yeast, salt. That's it. Once you've made your own bread you'll wonder why you ever stopped.

## Ingredients

- 500g strong white bread flour, plus extra for dusting
- 7g fast-action dried yeast (1 sachet)
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 300ml warm water

## Method

### 1. Mix the dough

Combine the flour, yeast and salt in a large bowl. Add the olive oil, then pour in the water a little at a time, mixing as you go. You want a rough, shaggy dough that comes together into a ball. It'll look a mess. That's fine.

### 2. Knead

Tip onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 10 minutes. Push, fold, turn. It should go from rough and sticky to smooth and elastic. If it's sticking badly, a little more flour — don't overdo it.

### 3. First rise

Shape into a ball, put back in the bowl, cover with a damp cloth or cling film and leave somewhere warmish for 1 to 1½ hours, until doubled in size.

### 4. Shape

Knock the air out with your fist. Shape into a rough oval — this is the bloomer. Put it on a lightly oiled baking tray. Slash the top a few times with a sharp knife. Cover loosely and leave to prove for another 30–45 minutes.

### 5. Bake

Preheat the oven to 220°C (200°C fan). Bake for 25–30 minutes until golden and the bottom sounds hollow when you tap it. Leave to cool on a wire rack before you tear into it. At least a bit.

## Why this works

Bread is one of those things that feels like it should be complicated. It isn't. Yeast does the hard work. You just need to let it get on with it.

The long prove develops flavour. The hot oven gives you the crust.

## Make it immense

- A handful of mixed seeds pressed into the top before the second prove.
- Rub the top with a little butter the moment it comes out of the oven.
- Same dough, divided into eight — makes excellent rolls. Bake for 15–18 minutes.

## No-faff swaps

- No strong white flour? Plain flour works but the texture won't be quite as chewy.
- No fast-action yeast? Active dried yeast is fine — activate it in the warm water with a pinch of sugar for 10 minutes first.
- Short on time? Slow-prove overnight in the fridge. Mix the dough in the evening, cover, leave it overnight, shape and bake in the morning.
