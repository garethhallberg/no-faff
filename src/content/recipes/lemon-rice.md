Start with properly cooked basmati rice, then make it interesting with lemon.

The ratio is easy: **80g rice per person, then double that in cold water**. For two people, that means 160g basmati rice and 320ml cold water.

## Ingredients

- 160g basmati rice
- 320ml cold water
- 1 lemon
- 1 tablespoon butter or olive oil
- Salt
- Black pepper

## Method

### 1. Measure the rice

Use 80g basmati rice per serving.

For two servings, put 160g rice in a saucepan with 320ml cold water and a pinch of salt.

### 2. Cook it

Bring the pan to the boil.

As soon as it boils, put the lid on, turn the heat right down, and cook for 10 minutes.

Do not stir it. Do not keep lifting the lid to check. Let the rice get on with it.

### 3. Rest it

Turn off the heat and leave the rice, lid still on, for a couple of minutes.

This finishes the cooking and helps the grains settle.

### 4. Lemon it

Grate in the zest of the lemon, then squeeze in the juice from half of it.

Add the butter or olive oil, a little black pepper, and fluff everything through with a fork.

Taste. Add more lemon juice if you want it sharper.

## Why this works

The rice cooks by absorption, so the water is measured rather than guessed. The lemon zest gives fragrance, the juice gives brightness, and the fat stops it feeling thin.

## Make it immense

- Add chopped parsley, coriander or dill.
- Stir in toasted almonds or pine nuts.
- Add a pinch of turmeric if you want colour.
- Serve with grilled halloumi, roasted vegetables, chickpeas or anything tomatoey.

## No-faff swaps

- No fresh lemon? Use lime.
- No butter? Olive oil is perfect.
- Want it punchier? Add a tiny grated garlic clove with the lemon.
