This starts with the same reliable basmati method: **80g rice per person, then double that in cold water**.

Then you fry mushrooms properly and fold them through at the end. Much better than boiling them into the rice and wondering why everything tastes grey.

## Ingredients

- 160g basmati rice
- 320ml cold water
- 250g mushrooms, sliced
- 1 tablespoon butter
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 small garlic clove, finely chopped or grated
- Salt
- Black pepper
- A small handful of parsley, chopped (optional)

## Method

### 1. Measure the rice

Use 80g basmati rice per serving.

For two servings, put 160g rice in a saucepan with 320ml cold water and a pinch of salt.

### 2. Cook it

Bring the pan to the boil.

As soon as it boils, put the lid on, turn the heat right down, and cook for 10 minutes.

Leave it alone while it cooks.

### 3. Fry the mushrooms

While the rice cooks, heat the olive oil in a frying pan over a medium-high heat.

Add the mushrooms with a pinch of salt and cook until they release their water, the water evaporates, and the mushrooms start to brown.

Add the butter, garlic and plenty of black pepper. Cook for another minute.

### 4. Rest the rice

Turn off the heat under the rice and leave it, lid still on, for a couple of minutes.

Fluff it with a fork.

### 5. Finish

Fold the fried mushrooms through the rice.

Add parsley if you have it, taste for salt and pepper, and serve.

## Why this works

The rice cooks cleanly by absorption, while the mushrooms get proper heat in a frying pan. That means fluffy rice and browned mushrooms, not damp mushroom porridge.

## Make it immense

- Add a splash of soy sauce to the mushrooms at the end.
- Use thyme instead of parsley.
- Finish with Parmesan.
- Add a squeeze of lemon if it needs lifting.

## No-faff swaps

- Any mushrooms work. Chestnut mushrooms are great.
- No fresh garlic? Leave it out, or use garlic oil.
- Want it richer? Add another knob of butter at the end.
