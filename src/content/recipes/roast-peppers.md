This is barely a recipe. It is more an instruction. Which is exactly the point.

## Ingredients

- 4 whole peppers (any colour, mix is nice)
- 1 head of garlic, broken into cloves, skins on
- Olive oil
- Salt

## Method

### 1. Heat the oven

180°C.

### 2. Brush the peppers

Whole peppers, in a roasting tin, brushed with olive oil. Don't be precious.

Throw the garlic cloves in alongside, skins still on.

### 3. Roast

40 minutes.

That's it.

## Why this works

Whole peppers steam inside their own skins as they roast. The flesh goes silky. The garlic goes sweet and squashable.

## Make it immense

Once cooled enough to handle, peel the peppers (the skins slip off), tear them into strips, and dress with:

- the soft garlic, squeezed out of its skin
- a splash of red wine vinegar
- good olive oil
- salt

Eat with bread. Or stir through pasta. Or pile onto toast.

## No-faff swaps

- Big peppers, small peppers, pointy peppers — all fine.
- No good olive oil? Any oil. The peppers do the work.
