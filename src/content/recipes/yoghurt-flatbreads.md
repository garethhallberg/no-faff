Soft, chewy flatbreads without yeast, fuss, or waiting around all afternoon. The yoghurt does the work.

## Ingredients

- 125g self-raising flour, plus extra for dusting
- About 125g natural Greek yoghurt
- Olive oil, for brushing
- A pinch of salt

## Method

### 1. Make the dough

Put the flour and salt in a bowl. Add roughly the same weight of Greek yoghurt and mix until it comes together into a soft dough.

If it feels too sticky, add a little more flour. If it feels dry, add a spoonful more yoghurt.

### 2. Rest

Cover the dough and leave it for 20 to 30 minutes.

### 3. Roll

Divide the dough into flatbreads and roll them out on a lightly floured surface.

Brush the top side with olive oil.

### 4. Grill

Heat a grill pan until hot.

Place the flatbread oil-side down in the pan, then brush the top side with oil.

Cook for a couple of minutes at most, until charred in places and puffed a little. Flip and cook the other side for another minute or two.

## No-faff notes

- Good with curry, soup, mezze, eggs, or whatever needs scooping.
- Garlic oil is excellent here.
- Make them smaller if the grill pan is crowded. They cook fast, so give them space.
