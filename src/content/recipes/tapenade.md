Tapenade sounds like something you have to buy in a tiny expensive jar.

You do not. You smash olives, capers and oil until it becomes the thing.

## Ingredients

- 1 jar of black olives, drained
- 1 tablespoon capers
- Plenty of extra virgin olive oil
- Salt and black pepper

## Method

### 1. Drain

Drain the olives. Drain the capers too if they are sitting in brine.

### 2. Smash

Put the olives and capers into a pestle and mortar.

Smash them until they break down into a rough paste.

### 3. Loosen

Add a good glug of extra virgin olive oil and keep working it. Add more oil until it looks right.

You want a loose paste, not olive cement.

### 4. Season

Taste it. Add black pepper and a little salt if it needs it.

Go carefully with the salt. Olives and capers usually bring plenty of their own.

## Why this works

Olives bring the deep, salty richness. Capers bring sharpness. Extra virgin olive oil makes it glossy and spoonable.

That is the whole trick.

## Make it immense

- A squeeze of lemon juice.
- A small clove of garlic.
- A few anchovies if you are not keeping it vegetarian.
- A pinch of chilli flakes.

## No-faff swaps

- In a hurry? Use a blender or mini chopper. Quicker and almost as good.
- No pestle and mortar? Chop everything by hand. It will be chunkier and excellent.
- Green olives work too, but the flavour will be sharper.
- No capers? Use a small splash of vinegar or lemon juice.

## Eat with

- Toast
- Crackers
- Roast vegetables
- Pasta
- Anything that needs a salty shove in the right direction
