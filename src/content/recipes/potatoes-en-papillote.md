Potatoes in a bag. Just perfect.

The paper traps the steam. The potatoes cook in their own moisture. Everything in the bag flavours everything else.

## Ingredients

- 500g new potatoes, halved if large
- A handful of mushrooms, halved or quartered
- 4 spring onions, cut into 2cm pieces
- 3 cloves garlic, smashed (skins off, but no need to chop)
- Olive oil
- Salt and pepper

## Method

### 1. Heat the oven

200°C.

### 2. Build the parcel

Tear off a big sheet of baking paper. Pile the potatoes, mushrooms, spring onions and garlic in the middle.

Glug of olive oil. Generous salt and pepper. Toss with your hands.

### 3. Seal it

Bring the edges of the paper up over the top and fold them down to seal. Then twist the ends like a Christmas cracker.

It doesn't need to be airtight — just closed enough to hold the steam in.

Put the parcel on a baking tray.

### 4. Bake

30 minutes. Don't open it to check. Trust the bag.

### 5. Open at the table

Tear it open at the table. The smell when it opens is the whole point.

## Why this works

It's steaming and roasting at the same time. The potatoes go fluffy inside but pick up flavour from the garlic and mushrooms. The spring onions go silky. Nothing dries out.

It's also basically zero washing up.

## Make it immense

- A sprig of thyme or rosemary inside the parcel.
- A splash of white wine before sealing.
- A knob of butter on top of everything before you close the bag.
- Crumbled blue cheese stirred through after opening.

## No-faff swaps

- No spring onions? A sliced shallot, or a small chopped leek.
- No mushrooms? Skip them. Add cherry tomatoes instead.
- No baking paper? Tin foil works fine. Less elegant, same result.
