Pesto is better when it has been bashed about a bit.

This one uses basil for sweetness, rocket for pepperiness, pine nuts for the creamy bit, and lemon to keep it bright.

## Ingredients

- A good handful of basil
- A good handful of rocket
- 1 small handful pine nuts
- 1 garlic clove
- Parmesan, grated
- 1/2 lemon
- Plenty of extra virgin olive oil
- A pinch of salt

## Method

### 1. Garlic

Put the garlic clove and a pinch of salt into a pestle and mortar.

Smash it into a paste.

### 2. Pine nuts

Add the pine nuts and bash them into the garlic.

You want them broken down, but not completely smooth.

### 3. Greens

Add the basil and rocket a bit at a time, working them into the paste.

It will look like too much leaf at first. Keep going.

### 4. Loosen

Add a good glug of extra virgin olive oil and stir it through.

Add more oil until it looks glossy and spoonable.

### 5. Finish

Stir in the grated parmesan and squeeze in the half lemon.

Taste it. More oil if it is too thick. More lemon if it needs waking up.

## Why this works

The salt helps break down the garlic. The pine nuts and parmesan give the pesto body. Basil brings the soft, sweet green flavour, rocket stops it being too polite, and lemon keeps the whole thing awake.

## Make it immense

- A little extra parmesan or pecorino.
- Lemon zest as well as juice.
- A few extra pine nuts on top.

## No-faff swaps

- No rocket? Use all basil.
- No pine nuts? Walnuts, almonds or cashews will do the job.
- Too thick? More olive oil.
- In a hurry? Use a blender or mini chopper at a push but don't over blend it. It's quicker nu not as good.

## Eat with

- Pasta
- Roast vegetables
- Toast
- Tomatoes
- Anything that wants a green slap of flavour
