Forget the mayo version. This is the proper one. The vinegar gets into the warm potatoes and does all the work.

## Ingredients

- 800g waxy potatoes (Charlotte, Anya, new potatoes — anything that holds together)
- 2 shallots, finely chopped
- 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
- 4 tablespoons good olive oil
- A small handful of parsley, finely chopped
- Salt and pepper

## Method

### 1. Cook the potatoes

Bring the potatoes to the boil in well-salted water.

Reduce the heat and simmer until tender — a knife should slide in without resistance. Usually 15 to 20 minutes depending on size.

### 2. Peel them warm

Drain. When cool enough to handle but still warm, slip the skins off with your fingers. They'll come away easily.

Slice into thick rounds.

### 3. Dress them warm

While still warm, mix together the shallots, red wine vinegar, olive oil and parsley. Pour over the potatoes and turn gently.

Season with salt and pepper.

### 4. Wait

Leave for at least half an hour. This is the bit people skip. Don't.

The warm potatoes drink up the dressing. You want them flavoured all the way through, not just on the surface.

## Why this works

Warm potato is porous. Cold potato is sealed. Dress them while they're warm and the vinegar gets right in. Skip this step and you've made a totally different, much worse salad.

## Make it immense

- A spoonful of Dijon mustard whisked into the dressing.
- Capers. Always capers.
- A handful of cornichons, sliced.
- Soft-boiled eggs on top, halved.

## No-faff swaps

- No shallots? A small amount of finely chopped red onion, rinsed under cold water first.
- No parsley? Chives, dill, tarragon. Any soft green herb.
- No red wine vinegar? White wine vinegar. Cider vinegar. Lemon juice at a pinch.
