This is the famous Marcella Hazan tomato sauce, done the no-faff way. Butter, onion, tomatoes, time.

That is basically it.

## Ingredients

- 4 blobs of butter
- 2 onions, peeled and halved
- 3 x 440g cans of good quality tomatoes
- Salt and black pepper

## Method

### 1. Butter

Put a large pan on the hob. Add the four blobs of butter to the base of the pan, spaced out a bit.

### 2. Onions

Put half an onion on top of each piece of butter, cut side down.

### 3. Tomatoes

Add the three cans of tomatoes. Squash them a bit if they are whole. Add salt and black pepper.

### 4. Cook

Cook gently on a low heat for 45 minutes. Stir now and then, mostly to make sure nothing is catching.

When it is done, lift out the onion halves. Or eat them. Your kitchen, your rules.

## Why this works

The butter rounds everything out. The onion perfumes the sauce without making you chop anything. The long, low cook takes the sharp edge off the tomatoes and turns the whole thing glossy.

## Make it immense

- Use the best tinned tomatoes you can justify.
- Finish the pasta in the sauce with a splash of pasta water.
- Parmesan on top, obviously.

## No-faff swaps

- No whole tomatoes? Chopped tomatoes are fine.
- No big onions? Use smaller ones and halve enough to cover the butter.
- No parmesan? Pecorino. Or nothing. The sauce can take care of itself.
